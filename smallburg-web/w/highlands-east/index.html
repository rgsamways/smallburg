Community profile

What we already know
about Highlands East.

History

Uranium, timber, and amalgamation

Cardiff and Bicroft were uranium mining communities — the Bicroft and Dyno mines opened in 1956 and closed within years. The municipality was formed January 1, 2001, through amalgamation of Bicroft, Cardiff, Glamorgan, and Monmouth townships. Like Bancroft, the community transitioned from resource extraction without collapse.

Economy

Seasonal tourism and trades

Wilberforce is the administrative hub. Tourism, cottaging, and outdoor recreation drive the seasonal economy. Local trades, forestry, and small business form the year-round base. Haliburton County provides upper-tier services. The municipality is one of four lower-tier municipalities in the County.

Leadership

Long-serving and stable

Mayor Dave Burton, 4th term as of 2022. CAO Brittany McCaw. Deputy Mayor Ryall. Council meets second Tuesday monthly at Lloyd Watson Memorial Community Centre, Wilberforce. Livestreamed on YouTube. CivicWeb platform. Active and accessible.

Geography

Four communities, one voice

Cardiff (historic mining), Wilberforce (administrative hub), Bicroft, and communities across Glamorgan and Monmouth wards. Ward-based representation ensures all corners of the 688 km² municipality have a seat at council.

Vulnerability

Post-mining identity transition

The Cardiff-Bicroft uranium mining heritage left a physical and economic legacy the community has spent 60+ years navigating. Knowledge of that era — land use, environmental constraints, mineral claim history — is concentrated in an aging generation with no digital capture mechanism.

Opportunity

Adjacent to Bancroft hub

Highlands East sits at the intersection of Haliburton County and North Hastings — drawing from both the Bancroft service hub and the Haliburton village hub. Positioned to benefit from Smallburg's presence in both counties as the platform expands.
